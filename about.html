Background

Growing up, I've always loved playing video games. Aside from being an astronaut, a video game developer was my dream job as a kid.

It was around my junior year of high school I decided I wanted to get into film-making. I took a class on screenwriting and film broadcasting and I was convinced it was something I wanted to do. It wasn't until my senior year of high school when I first took a programming class. I had a natural talent for it but my heart was still set on film-making.

In 2014, I was accepted into Eastern Michigan University to study Electronic Media and Film Theory with a minor in Computer Science. As my enrollment continued, I found myself enjoying the computer science courses much more than the film theory classes, I decided to change my major to Simulation, Animation, and Gaming.

The courses I took revolved around 3d design, learning to use different software programs, and coding. I started dating my who would later become my wife. I participated in the marching band playing the trombone. I also got my first job in food service while on campus.

In 2019, I spent my last semester at EMU commuting to school twice a week. I began practicing game design in Unity and I started working at Taco Bell. After graduating college I would spend much of my free time learning to develop games in Unity using the skills I learned in college as well as teaching myself new skills.

Since then, I've gotten married, I've participated in 12 game jams and worked on several personal Unity projects. I've become a manager at Taco Bell, and I had a son in May of 2022.

With all of my experience using Unity, I've come to the conclusion that coding is my favorite aspect of game design. I joined Coding Temple in January of 2023 with the hopes of learning new programming skills and a start to my career as a software developer!
